Genres

R&B (Rhythm and Blues) is a genre of popular music that originated in African American communities in the 1940s. It blends elements of jazz, gospel, and blues, characterized by smooth vocals, soulful melodies, and a strong backbeat.

Rap is a musical genre that emerged in the 1970s as part of hip-hop culture in African American and Latino communities in New York City. It features rhythmic spoken word or "rapping," often delivered over beats or sampled music. Lyrically, rap covers a wide range of topics, from social and political issues to personal experiences, storytelling, and self-expression.

Indie pop is a genre that blends the melodic, catchy elements of pop music with the DIY ethos of independent (indie) music. Emerging in the 1980s, it is characterized by its bright, jangly guitar sounds, upbeat rhythms, and often introspective or quirky lyrics.
